## Competitive Marketing Analysis Framework

This framework provides the analytical structure used throughout the competitor monitoring flow. It defines the dimensions of analysis, classification systems, and evaluation criteria for consistent competitive intelligence.

### The Five Pillars of Competitive Marketing Analysis

#### Pillar 1: Market Positioning

How a competitor defines their place in the market relative to alternatives.

**Analysis Dimensions:**
- **Category Definition:** What category do they claim to compete in? Do they attempt to create a new category?
- **Target Segment:** Who is their primary audience? (by company size, industry, role, geography)
- **Price Positioning:** Where do they sit on the value-premium spectrum?
- **Competitive Frame:** Who do they position against? (named competitors, generic alternatives, the status quo)
- **Differentiation Basis:** What is their claimed unique advantage? (technology, price, service, brand, network effect)

**Evaluation Questions:**
- Is their positioning clear and consistent across channels?
- Does their actual offering match their positioning claims?
- Are they positioned against us, and if so, on what basis?

#### Pillar 2: Messaging Architecture

The structure and content of a competitor's marketing communications.

**Analysis Dimensions:**
- **Value Proposition:** The core promise (what they deliver, to whom, and why it matters)
- **Messaging Pillars:** 3-5 recurring themes that support the value proposition
- **Proof Points:** Evidence used to substantiate claims (case studies, metrics, awards, testimonials)
- **Emotional Positioning:** The feelings their messaging evokes (trust, excitement, urgency, belonging)
- **Brand Voice:** The personality and tone of their communications

**Evaluation Questions:**
- Do their messaging pillars address genuine customer needs or are they feature-driven?
- How strong is their proof? Can we challenge it?
- Is there a messaging gap we can own?

#### Pillar 3: Channel Strategy

Where and how a competitor reaches their audience.

**Analysis Dimensions:**
- **Owned Channels:** Website, blog, email newsletter, community
- **Social Platforms:** Which platforms, posting frequency, engagement levels
- **Paid Channels:** Search ads, social ads, display, sponsorships
- **Earned Media:** Press coverage, analyst mentions, review sites, awards
- **Events & Partnerships:** Conference presence, partner ecosystem, integrations

**Evaluation Questions:**
- Where are they investing most heavily? (indicates strategic priority)
- Where are they absent or weak? (potential opportunity for us)
- How does their channel mix compare to ours?

#### Pillar 4: Campaign Execution

How a competitor brings marketing programmes to market.

**Analysis Dimensions:**
- **Campaign Themes:** What narratives are they building?
- **Campaign Cadence:** How frequently do they launch new initiatives?
- **Creative Quality:** Production value, design quality, copywriting sophistication
- **Conversion Mechanics:** How do they capture and nurture leads?
- **Innovation:** Are they using new channels, formats, or tactics?

**Evaluation Questions:**
- What is their campaign hit rate? (proportion that gains traction vs. falls flat)
- Are they testing aggressively or playing it safe?
- What can we learn from their approach without copying it?

#### Pillar 5: Market Signals

Observable indicators of a competitor's strategic direction and health.

**Signal Categories:**
- **Growth Signals:** Hiring velocity, office expansion, funding, customer announcements
- **Product Signals:** Feature releases, pricing changes, new integrations, patent filings
- **Strategy Signals:** Leadership changes, rebranding, market exits/entries, partnership shifts
- **Distress Signals:** Layoffs, customer complaints, executive departures, pricing desperation
- **Market Signals:** Regulatory changes, technology shifts, economic conditions affecting the competitive landscape

### Threat Severity Matrix

| | Low Impact | Medium Impact | High Impact |
|---|-----------|---------------|------------|
| **Confirmed** | Monitor | Plan response | Act immediately |
| **Likely** | Note | Plan response | Prepare contingency |
| **Speculative** | Log | Monitor for confirmation | Track closely |

### Monitoring Cadence Guidelines

| Market Dynamics | Full Audit | Signal Check | Report |
|----------------|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Fast-moving (SaaS, tech) | Quarterly | Weekly | Fortnightly |
| Moderate (professional services) | Semi-annually | Fortnightly | Monthly |
| Stable (established industries) | Annually | Monthly | Quarterly |
